What you're watching

A discrete event simulation of an M/G/c queueing system. Events — arrivals and service completions — are advanced in simulated time and logged as they occur. The floor view on the left renders each event as a customer moving between the door, the queue, and the servers.

The stability rule

The traffic intensity is ρ = λ / (c · μ). When ρ < 1, average queue length is finite and the system is stable. As ρ → 1, variability dominates: waits blow up nonlinearly — especially with high service variance.

Little's Law

The average number in the queue equals arrival rate times average wait: Lq = λ · Wq. Compare the live measurement against the theoretical M/M/c Erlang-C estimate shown above each KPI.
