Controls

Every vehicle arrives through an event queue. Faster followers trigger catch-up events when they run down a safe gap, which is where shockwaves begin.

Tip: push inflow upward until density rises faster than speed can recover. The flow-density scatter on the lower left will bend over when the roadway nears capacity.

    Model notes

    This is intentionally lightweight: vehicles have piecewise-constant speed between events, arrivals follow a Poisson process, and lane changes are omitted so you can see event timing clearly. Congestion forms when faster followers repeatedly hit safe-gap thresholds behind slower leaders.

    Road length: 1.5 km per lane. Passenger cars are 4.5 m long, trucks 17 m. Safe following distance grows with speed and braking noise, so uncertainty makes waves travel upstream sooner.
